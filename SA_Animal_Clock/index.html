Methodology & Notes

  1. Poultry & Male Chicks: Counter reflects broiler chickens slaughtered for meat (~1.125 Billion). Data derived from the SAPA Board Report 2024. Note: This excludes the millions of male chicks culled (killed at birth) in the egg industry, as they are not raised for food consumption.
  2. Red Meat (Cattle, Pigs, Sheep): Data sourced from official levy statistics (RMIS) and the BFAP 2025 Trajectories report. Numbers reflect local slaughter volumes adjusted for live exports.
  3. Ostriches: Data based on the Western Cape Department of Agriculture's Industry Footprint report and recent industry slaughter forecasts (~150k annually).
  4. Crocodiles: 72,750 crocodiles harvested annually (2016-2018 avg). The industry is primarily driven by the skin trade, with meat utilized as a byproduct for local consumption and export. Sources: South Africa Online, CITES Trade Data.
  5. Wild-Caught Fish: SA commercial fisheries report catch in Metric Tons (approx. 600,000t/year). To estimate lives, we divide species tonnage by average individual weight. Dominated by Anchovy (152,000t) and Sardine. Note: This figure excludes bycatch (discarded non-target marine life), which academic research (e.g., Attwood et al.) estimates at ~15% of inshore trawl volume.
  6. Farmed Fish: Based on StatsSA Report 13-00-00 (2023) showing ~9,800 tons of production. This sector is dominated by Mussels and Abalone (approx. 90% of value). Individual life counts for shellfish are conservative estimates derived from total production tonnage minus shell weight to approximate living tissue weight.
